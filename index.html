  • When KOLs promote Hybrid, 25% of buyers end up purchasing Diamond (upgrade), with ROAS 7.53 — higher than the 5.2 ROAS when buyers match exactly. This suggests Hybrid content may set effective price anchoring.
  • Zero Gravity promotions consistently result in Diamond sales rather than ZG itself (6 KOLs, €118K). ZG may be driving awareness and aspiration but Diamond's price point sees higher conversion.
  • 30.7% of promoted KOLs generated €0 SSMAD revenue in the tracked period — Diamond (51 KOLs) and Hybrid (42 KOLs) account for the majority. Consider tightening onboarding criteria or post-launch follow-up protocols.
  • Accessories downgrade patterns (Hybrid→Acc, Diamond→Acc) still show reasonable ROAS (2.4–6.4), meaning these audiences are purchasing, just lower-value items — potentially bedding or accessories cross-sells.
  • Only 11 KOLs promoted Zero Gravity, limiting conclusions on ZG strategy. More dedicated ZG content from higher-reach KOLs could test true demand at this price tier.

  • Lifestyle, Foreigner, and Entertainment are the top three categories by revenue (€1M, €980K, €675K), collectively accounting for over 75% of all tracked IM revenue. These three should remain the core content strategy.
  • Technology KOLs have the highest Zero Gravity affinity (19.2% ZG share) and Travel KOLs favor Diamond (40.7%) — both categories index above average for premium product mix, suggesting purchase-ready audiences.
  • Daily News KOLs show an unusual 81.3% Diamond concentration with low ROAS (0.77) — the category may capture aspirational audiences without strong conversion intent.
  • Lifestyle on YouTube dramatically outperforms Lifestyle on Instagram (ROAS 11.67 vs 0.38) — the same category behaves very differently by platform, indicating content format matters as much as topic.
  • Niche categories like Comedy, Pets, and Medical have small KOL pools but above-average ROAS (10.27, 4.19, 2.38) — these audiences appear more targeted and conversion-ready.
  • Categories like Music, Beauty, and Fashion have minimal revenue and near-zero ROAS — limited purchase intent alignment with sleep products, even where some product sales occurred.
